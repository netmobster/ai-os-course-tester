This System Works. Here's What Will Still Frustrate You.

The system you built is real and it works. What follows isn't a warning that you did it wrong — it's an honest map of the walls people hit after they have a working AI OS. You will hit some of these. Probably not all of them. But naming them now means you recognize them when they show up instead of concluding that something's broken.

THE WALLS

WALL 1 — PROFILE DRIFT
The profile that was accurate in month one isn't quite right in month four. Your work changed, your role changed, something about how you operate shifted — and the AI is still responding to who you were, not who you are now.
Fix: run a profile update session. Re-run the Unit 2 interview with yourself. Diff the new answers against the old profile. Update what's stale. This is maintenance, not failure — it means the system is calibrated closely enough that drift is noticeable.
WALL 2 — CONTEXT CEILING
You've been doing the closing ritual. State documents exist. But the AI still can't carry context across sessions the way you wish it could — you're pasting the state doc at session open and it's working, but it feels manual. You want it to just know.
This is a real limitation of how current AI tools work, not a system design problem. The ritual bridges it as well as it can be bridged today. Tab 3 has one path forward if this is the thing that's most frustrating.
WALL 3 — GEAR GAP
You find yourself doing something repeatedly that doesn't fit cleanly into Interview or Handoff. A recurring type of work — research passes, meeting prep, writing mode, whatever — that needs a different posture and you're reinventing it every time.
Build the gear. You have the pattern from Unit 4. Name it, define the trigger, define what it excludes, define the exit. Add it to your system prompt. This is the system expanding to fit you — which is exactly what it's designed to do.
WALL 4 — THE PROFILE IS STILL TOO THIN
Six units in, the profile describes your wiring. It doesn't describe your history, your career context, the specific relationships that matter in your work, the current mission and its real constraints. The AI knows how you operate. It doesn't fully know what you're operating on.
This is the most common wall. The profile you built is a foundation, not a ceiling. The next upgrade is making it richer — and there's a specific tool for that.
